Sensitive Skin

Morning Routine: In the morning, start by cleansing your face with a very mild, fragrance-free hydrating cleanser that doesn't strip your skin's natural barrier. Follow this with a soothing toner or facial mist that’s alcohol-free—look for calming ingredients like chamomile or aloe vera. Apply a lightweight moisturizer with ingredients like ceramides or hyaluronic acid to maintain hydration and strengthen the skin barrier. Finish with a broad-spectrum sunscreen of SPF 30 or higher that’s specifically formulated for sensitive skin—preferably mineral-based with zinc oxide or titanium dioxide, which are less likely to cause irritation.

Night Routine: At night, remove any makeup or sunscreen with a gentle, oil-based cleansing balm or micellar water designed for sensitive skin. Follow up with the same mild cleanser used in the morning to ensure your skin is clean without over-washing. Apply a calming and nourishing night cream or facial oil with ingredients like squalane, calendula, or oat extract to soothe irritation and support overnight healing. Lastly, use a hypoallergenic lip balm to keep your lips soft and protected through the night. Avoid harsh treatments, exfoliants, or fragrances, as sensitive skin reacts best to minimal, gentle care.

Dry Skin

Morning Routine: In the morning, start by cleansing your face with a gentle hydrating cleanser to remove any impurities that may have built up overnight. Follow it up with a hydrating toner or mist to refresh and prep your skin. Next, apply a thick moisturizer enriched with hyaluronic acid to lock in moisture and keep your skin plump throughout the day. Finish your routine with a broad-spectrum moisturizing sunscreen of SPF 30 or higher to protect your skin from harmful UV rays.

Night Routine: At night, begin by removing any makeup and sunscreen residue with a cleansing balm, ensuring your skin is clean without stripping its natural oils. Then, use a cream-based cleanser to further cleanse and soothe your skin. Apply a nourishing night cream or a facial oil to help repair and hydrate your skin while you sleep. Don’t forget to apply a lip balm to keep your lips soft and moisturized overnight.

Normal Skin

Morning Routine: In the morning, start by cleansing your face with a gentle hydrating cleanser to remove any impurities and refresh your skin. Follow this with a hydrating toner or facial mist to balance your skin's moisture levels. Next, apply a thick yet lightweight moisturizer containing hyaluronic acid to maintain hydration and keep your skin smooth throughout the day. Finish your morning routine with a broad-spectrum moisturizing sunscreen with SPF 30 or higher to protect your skin from sun damage and maintain a healthy glow.

Night Routine: At night, begin by removing makeup and any leftover sunscreen with a gentle cleansing balm that melts away impurities without over-drying your skin. Follow up with a cream-based cleanser to ensure a thorough yet soothing cleanse. Then, apply a nourishing night cream or a light facial oil to help your skin recover and stay hydrated while you sleep. Lastly, use a lip balm to keep your lips soft and moisturized overnight, ensuring they stay smooth and comfortable.

Oily Skin

Morning Routine: In the morning, cleanse your face with a gentle foaming or gel-based cleanser that helps remove excess oil and keeps your skin feeling fresh without stripping away natural moisture. Follow this with a lightweight, alcohol-free toner or facial mist to balance your skin’s pH and control shine. Apply an oil-free, water-based moisturizer that contains hyaluronic acid to hydrate without clogging pores. Finish your morning routine with a broad-spectrum sunscreen with SPF 30 or higher—preferably a matte or gel-based formula—to protect your skin from sun damage while keeping it shine-free.

Night Routine: At night, start by removing makeup and excess oil with a cleansing balm or micellar water that’s suitable for oily skin. Follow up with a gentle gel or foaming cleanser to ensure your pores are thoroughly cleansed. After cleansing, apply a lightweight, non-comedogenic night gel or serum that contains ingredients like niacinamide or salicylic acid to help balance oil production and support overnight skin repair. End your routine with a simple lip balm to keep your lips moisturized and smooth through the night.

Combination Skin

Morning Routine: In the morning, cleanse your face with a gentle, balancing cleanser that effectively removes excess oil from the T-zone without drying out the cheeks. After cleansing, apply a refreshing toner or facial mist to even out skin tone and prep your face for hydration. Use a lightweight moisturizer that hydrates dry areas while being non-greasy enough for oil-prone zones—look for ingredients like hyaluronic acid or glycerin. Finish your routine with a broad-spectrum sunscreen with SPF 30 or higher, ideally a formula that’s hydrating yet non-comedogenic, to protect your skin without clogging pores or causing shine.

Night Routine: At night, begin by removing any makeup and buildup with a mild cleansing balm or micellar water. Follow this with a gentle cream or gel-based cleanser that addresses both dry and oily areas. Apply a nourishing night cream or serum that offers hydration without being too rich—this helps repair and balance the skin overnight. If your skin feels dry in some spots, you can layer a bit more moisturizer there. Don’t forget to apply a lip balm before bed to keep your lips soft and moisturized.
